6 MOST PROMINENT ANIMALS YOU CAN FIND IN EACH ECOSYSTEM

FOREST

COMMON NAME: White-Tailed Deer

Role: Herbivore
Habitat: Forests, fields, and brushy areas.
Range: White-tailed deer are found across North America, from southern Canada to South America.
Role in the Ecosystem: Seed Dispersal, Vegetation Control

COMMON NAME: American Black Bear

Role: Omnivore
Habitat: Forested areas
Range: Across North America
Role in the Ecosystem: Control Species Population, Disperse Seeds, Aerate Soil

COMMON NAME: Jaguar

Role: Apex Predator
Habitat: Dense rainforests, swamps, scrublands
Range: From southern United States through Central America to northern Argentina
Role in the Ecosystem: Controls prey populations, maintains ecological balance

COMMON NAME: Howler Monkey

Role: Herbivores
Habitat: Tropical Rainforests
Range: Central and South America
Role in the Ecosystem: Seed dispersers, contribute to forest regeneration

COMMON NAME: Toucan

Role: Frugivore
Habitat: Tropical Rainforests
Range: Central and South America
Role in the Ecosystem: Seed disperser

COMMON NAME: Eastern Gray Squirrel

Role: Herbivore
Habitat: Deciduous forests, urban areas, parks
Range: Eastern and Midwestern United States, parts of Canada
Role in the Ecosystem: Seed disperser, contributes to forest regeneration through caching behavior

GRASSLAND

COMMON NAME: American Bison

Role: Grazing Herbivore
Habitat: Prairies and Plains
Range: North America
Role in the Ecosystem: Influences plant community structure and soil health, maintains balance of vegetation, and creates habitats for other species.

COMMON NAME: African Elephant

Role: Large Herbivore
Habitat: Savannas and Grasslands
Range: Sub-Saharan Africa
Role in the Ecosystem: Shapes landscape by uprooting trees and creating water holes, maintains grassland structure, and benefits other wildlife through its feeding behavior.

COMMON NAME: Lion

Role: Apex Predator
Habitat: Savannas and Grasslands
Range: Sub-Saharan Africa
Role in the Ecosystem: Regulates populations of herbivores, maintains ecological balance by controlling prey species, which affects vegetation dynamics.

COMMON NAME: Plains Zebra

Role: Grazing Herbivore
Habitat: Savannas and Open Woodlands
Range: Eastern and Southern Africa
Role in the Ecosystem: Contributes to grassland health by grazing on grasses, promotes plant diversity, and serves as prey for large predators.

COMMON NAME: Pronghorn Antelope

Role: Grazing Herbivore
Habitat: Prairies and Grasslands
Range: Western and Central North America
Role in the Ecosystem: Maintains plant diversity by feeding on various vegetation, aids in seed dispersal, and provides prey for predators.

COMMON NAME: Saiga Antelope

Role: Grazing Herbivore
Habitat: Steppes
Range: Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan, Mongolia, and parts of Russia
Role in the Ecosystem: Influences vegetation patterns, helps maintain plant diversity, and serves as prey for predators like wolves.

DESERT

COMMON NAME: Dromedary Camel

Role: Large Herbivore
Habitat: Deserts, Arid Regions
Range: Middle East, Africa, South Asia
Role in the Ecosystem: Seed dispersal, transportation and sustenance (milk, meat), and vegetation dynamics

COMMON NAME: Fennec Fox

Role: Omnivore
Habitat: Deserts, Sand Dunes
Range: Sahara Desert, North Africa
Role in the Ecosystem: Controls insect and small mammal populations, disperses seeds, and serves as prey for larger predators

COMMON NAME: Sidewinder Rattlesnake

Role: Carnivore
Habitat: Sandy Deserts
Range: Southwestern US, Northwestern Mexico
Role in the Ecosystem: Predates on small mammals, birds, and lizards, helping to control their populations, and serves as prey for larger predators like birds of prey.

COMMON NAME: Desert Tortoise

Role: Herbivore
Habitat: Deserts, Arid Scrublands
Range: Southwestern US, Northwestern Mexico
Role in the Ecosystem: Influences plant communities through grazing, aids in seed dispersal, and creates burrows that provide shelter for other animals.

COMMON NAME: Kangaroo Rat

Role: Seed Disperser, Herbivore
Habitat: Deserts, Arid Regions
Range: Southwestern United States, Mexico
Role in the Ecosystem: Disperses seeds and nuts, aiding in plant propagation, and serves as prey for predators like owls, snakes, and coyotes, maintaining the desert food web.

COMMON NAME: Meerkat

Role: Omnivore
Habitat: Deserts, Arid Plains
Range: Southern Africa, Kalahari Desert, Namib Desert
Role in the Ecosystem: Controls insect and small vertebrate populations, aerates soil Controls insect and small vertebrate populations, aerates soil through burrowing, and serves as prey for larger predators, contributing to the ecological balance in desert habitats.

AQUATIC

COMMON NAME: Great White Shark

Role: Apex Predator
Habitat: Coastal and Offshore Waters
Range: Worldwide, in temperate and tropical oceans
Role in the Ecosystem: Regulates populations of marine mammals, fish, and other sharks, maintaining the balance of marine ecosystems and promoting healthy fish populations.

COMMON NAME: Blue Whale

Role: Filter Feeder
Habitat: Open Ocean
Range: Worldwide, in all major oceans
Role in the Ecosystem: Consumes large quantities of krill, playing a crucial role in the marine food web; contributes to nutrient cycling through fecal plumes that fertilize ocean waters.

COMMON NAME: Clownfish

Role: Omnivore
Habitat: Coral Reefs
Range: Indo-Pacific region, including the Great Barrier Reef and the Red Sea
Role in the Ecosystem: Forms symbiotic relationships with sea anemones, providing protection from predators and aiding in anemone health by cleaning and providing nutrients.

COMMON NAME: Sea Otter

Role: Keystone Species
Habitat: Coastal Waters, particularly kelp forests
Range: North Pacific Ocean
Role in the Ecosystem: Controls sea urchin populations, preventing overgrazing of kelp forests, which supports biodiversity and the structure of the ecosystem.

COMMON NAME: Emperor Penguin

Role: Predator
Habitat: Antarctic Ice and Surrounding Ocean
Range: Antarctic region
Role in the Ecosystem: Preys on fish, squid, and krill, playing a vital role in the Antarctic food web.

COMMON NAME: Green Sea Turtle

Role: Herbivore
Habitat: Coastal Waters
Range: Tropical and subtropical oceans worldwide
Role in the Ecosystem: Grazes on seagrass, maintaining healthy seagrass beds; aids in nutrient cycling between marine and terrestrial ecosystems during nesting.

TUNDRA

COMMON NAME: Polar Bear

Role: Apex Predator
Habitat: Arctic Tundra, Sea Ice
Range: Arctic Circle, primarily in the Arctic Ocean and surrounding lands
Role in the Ecosystem: Top predator, regulates populations of seals and other marine mammals, helps maintain balance in marine ecosystems.

COMMON NAME: Arctic Fox

Role: Predator and Scavenger
Habitat: Arctic Tundra, Coastal Areas
Range: Arctic regions (North America, Europe, and Asia)
Role in the Ecosystem: Hunts small mammals and birds, scavenges carcasses, helps control rodent populations.

COMMON NAME: Caribou (Reindeer)

Role: Herbivore
Habitat: Arctic Tundra, Boreal Forests
Range: Arctic and Subarctic regions of North America, Europe, and Asia
Role in the Ecosystem: Grazes on lichens, mosses, and shrubs, important prey for predators, contributes to nutrient cycling.

COMMON NAME: Arctic Hare

Role: Herbivore
Habitat: Arctic Tundras
Range: Arctic regions of North America and Greenland
Role in the Ecosystem: Feeds on grasses, lichens, and other vegetation, preyed upon by predators, contributes to nutrient cycling.

COMMON NAME: Snowy Owl

Role: Apex Predator
Habitat: Arctic Tundra, Coastal Areas
Range: Arctic regions (North America, Europe, and Asia)
Role in the Ecosystem: Hunts small mammals and birds, helps control rodent populations, plays a role in the food web dynamics.

COMMON NAME: Lemming

Role: Prey Species
Habitat: Arctic Tundra
Range: Arctic regions of North America, Europe, and Asia
Role in the Ecosystem: Primary food source for predators such as Arctic foxes and snowy owls, influences vegetation dynamics through grazing and burrowing behaviors.
